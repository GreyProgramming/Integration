Be Whoever You Want To Be.

For a long time, D&D had a reputation for being a cis-het white man's game, but since the advent of 5e and online platforms such as Roll20 and Fantasy Grounds, the game's demographics have taken a huge shift towards a more diverse player base. more women, people of colour and queer players are being drawn to the game.

This is by no means an accident - in the recently released Waterdeep: Dungeon of the Mad Mage, there is a questline where you return an NPC to her wife, an obvious LGBT storyline.

Further, roleplaying games allow you to explore ideas you have about yourself in a safe fantasy environment, so you can see how various aspects of the character interract and how you feel about them.
